Hammond PSD Water System Failures

1. Massive Water Loss

Hammond PSD loses over 1.8 million gallons per month—about 30% of all water pumped. This translates to tens of thousands of dollars in waste every year with no meaningful system upgrades to show for it.

2. Chronic Leaks and Breaks

The system averaged 35–40 leaks and 4–5 major line breaks per year. These figures reflect ongoing infrastructure failure with little preventative action.

3. Serious Health Violations

Hammond PSD received two Tier 1 violations in both 2022 and 2023—meaning urgent public health threats like bacterial contamination or system pressure loss that require 24-hour public notice.

4. Maintenance Failures

In 2024, the district logged 240 hours of emergency maintenance and only 65 hours of preventative maintenance—showing they spend nearly 4x more time reacting than preventing.

5. Water Advisories and Contamination Risk

Emergency planning documents outline scenarios where residents must boil water, avoid drinking it, or even avoid bathing or touching it. Several real events have triggered these warnings.

6. SCADA System Still Missing

Since 2019, Hammond PSD has claimed to be installing automated leak and pressure detection systems (SCADA). But no audit, budget, or board report shows the system was ever completed.

7. Minimal Reservoir Capacity & Pressure Drops

The system’s main reservoir holds only 0.12 million gallons—too little for sustained outages—and some zones experience dangerous pressure drops that allow contamination to enter the system.

Conclusion: Hammond PSD’s water infrastructure is failing. Water is leaking, pressure is dropping, health risks are real—and the money meant to fix it is going elsewhere. Ratepayers deserve better.
